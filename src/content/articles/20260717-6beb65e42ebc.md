<p>Poland’s Prime Minister Donald Tusk on Saturday announced the creation of a new monument to civilian victims of what he called a "genocide" by "Ukrainian nationalists" in World War II.</p>
<p>波蘭總理唐納德．圖斯克週六宣布，將為第二次世界大戰期間遭他所稱「烏克蘭民族主義者」所犯下的「種族滅絕」暴行的平民受害者興建一座新紀念碑。</p>

<p>Warsaw and Kyiv have been at loggerheads for decades over the massacre of civilians on both sides during World War II, souring relations.</p>
<p>數十年來，華沙與基輔一直因二戰期間雙方平民遭屠殺問題爭執不休，令兩國關係蒙上陰影。</p>
<p>But the dispute has taken on more significance given Poland’s support for its neighbour as Ukraine fights off an invasion by Russia.</p>
<p>不過，在波蘭持續支持鄰國烏克蘭抵抗俄羅斯入侵的背景下，這場歷史爭議如今更加受到關注。</p>
<p>Tusk, a former president of the European Council and a staunch backer of Ukraine against Russia, said "memory and truth must help us build a better future, without hatred and without contempt".</p>
<p>曾任歐洲理事會主席的圖斯克，也是堅定支持烏克蘭對抗俄羅斯侵略的政治人物。他表示：「記憶與真相應幫助我們建立一個更美好、沒有仇恨、沒有輕蔑的未來。」</p>
<p>"The Europe of peace and mutual respect, the Europe reconciled after World War II, was made possible thanks to truth and to calling things by their name."</p>
<p>他並表示：「和平與相互尊重的歐洲，戰後和解的歐洲，正是因為人們勇於面對真相，並如實為歷史定名。」</p>
<p>at loggerheads：慣用語，指爭執不下、僵持對立。例句：The two political parties have been at loggerheads over the new tax policy for months.（兩大政黨就新稅制政策已經爭執不下數個月。）</p>
<p>call things by their proper names：慣用語，指如實稱呼事物。例句：We must call things by their name and admit that innocent civilians were massacred.（我們必須如實面對事實，承認無辜平民遭到了屠殺。）</p>
