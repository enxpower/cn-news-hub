<p>The US economy picked up steam in the beginning of the year as the United States and Israel launched a destabilizing war with Iran that has jacked up prices and is still ongoing.</p>
<p>隨著美國與以色列對伊朗發動戰爭，導致區域動盪與物價陡升，今年第1季的美國經濟逐漸成長。</p>

<p>Gross domestic product, which measures all the goods and services produced in the economy, registered a 2% annualized rate in the January-through-March period, the Commerce Department said Thursday, up sharply from the fourth quarter’s 0.5%.</p>
<p>美國商務部週四表示，今年1月至3月期間，衡量經濟中所有商品與服務產出的國內生產毛額（GDP）年化增長率，從（去年）第4季的0.5％急升到2％。</p>
<p>US economic growth in the first quarter was boosted by resilient consumer spending, a massive uptick in business investment, higher exports (which contribute to GDP), and government outlays that came back online after the longest government shutdown on record in the prior quarter.</p>
<p>美國第1季的經濟成長，受益於持續穩定的消費者支出、企業投資大增、（對GDP有所貢獻的）出口有所成長，以及政府開支在經歷前1季的史上最長政府停擺後恢復正常。</p>

<p>jack up：片語，用千斤頂頂起來；（突然大幅）漲（價）。例句：A lot of hotels jack up their room rates during peak seasons.（許多飯店在旺季大漲房價。）</p>
